French comprehensible input for learners

Why I'm doing this

I have a passion for knowledge sharing and I love learning new languages. I've been learning Japanese by myself for a few years now, and after hitting a plateau, I found motivation again thanks to the "comprehensible input" method. I combined my passions for language learning and knowledge sharing with my writing skills to offer the same experience to French learners.

What I'm actually doing

Lis et écris en français ! (French/English) - I'm publishing content for French learners to help them improve their reading and writing skills in particular. I'm not writing lessons, but instead give advice on how to practice French without studying with the "comprehensible input" aka "language acquisition" method.

Here are my current inititiatives:

  • Mainly busy building a community and trying different ideas to find how I can actually help French learners using this method.
  • I'm sharing advice on how to use the "comprehensible input" method to learn French on my website "Lis et écris en français !".
  • I'm writing a "personal journal" kind of publication on Substack to share weekly short, funny (I'm trying at least) stories that French learners can use to practice reading in French. I try to come with interesting insight about the language with each story.
  • I chose to only use Pinterest as a social network to increase visibility, as I now refuse to use any media owned by Meta or X. Being active on Pinterest motivates me to create more visual content for my posts, which in turn improves visibility on search engines, so it's a win-win situation.
  • I'm giving Reddit a try. I'm actively answering questions from French learners on existing subs, and created my own (not yet very active). Although the community is not growing yet, this Reddit sub increased my visibility, as I gained subscribers and visitors directly from it.
  • I wrote one long-form content (ebook), as one of my end goals is to publish more of that kind of content. Before spending time on all my ebooks ideas, I created a first one to check if people are interested in this format and in hope to receive some feedback to improve the next ones.

On hiatus

I started writing The Code She Writes (English) when I changed career in 2020 (from a former 10-years administrative assistant career to working as a developer). That blog had a double-purpose:

  • "Learn by teaching": there's evidence that explaining things to others helps improve one's knowledge and notice gaps (cfr the Feynman technique for example).
  • Be a "woman in tech": as there is an under-representation of women in tech fields, I wanted to have a public visibility as a woman shifting to a career in IT.

(Also, it helps me find information I've previously seen without going through my favorite links, my Trello boards, my notebooks and so on.)

It's currently on a hiatus, but I still have ideas for it. To be even more transparent, I'm currently figuring out how I'm going to reshape my career in IT, and this blog will be my portfolio. So, before I start reworking it, I have to define a clear plan for myself!
